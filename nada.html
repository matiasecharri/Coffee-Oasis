1. A description of the problem: Explain in detail the problem, such as whether the unit is blowing warm air, making strange noises, or not turning on at all.

  2. The make and model of your air conditioning unit: Providing the make and model of your unit can help the technician determine the specific components that may be causing the problem.
  3. The age of your air conditioning unit: The age of your unit can give the technician an idea of how worn specific components may be and help them determine whether a repair or replacement may be more appropriate.
  4. The last time the unit was serviced: If you can recall the last time the unit was serviced, this can give the technician an idea of what maintenance has been performed and whether any components may need to be replaced.
  5. Your availability for an appointment: Be prepared to provide several times for the technician to come and make the repairs.
